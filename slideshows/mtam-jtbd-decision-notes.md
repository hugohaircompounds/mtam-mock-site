# JTBD Decision Notes — MTAM Homepage Demo

This document proves that every section of the MoreThanAMethod homepage demo exists because a Jobs-To-Be-Done framework decision put it there. Nothing on the page is decorative by default — each section traces back to a specific JTBD principle and a deliberate design rationale.

The demo applies six core JTBD frameworks: the Job Map Framework (universal sequence of customer progress), Four-Dimensional Job Analysis (functional, emotional, social, and identity job dimensions), the JTBD Anti-Vanity Framework (every element must serve a job, not look impressive), Outcome-Based Messaging Strategy (lead with what the customer achieves), JTBD for Information Architecture (organize by top tasks, not by org structure), and the Customer Success Language Model (convert platform language into customer language).

The page follows the Job Map's universal sequence — Define, Locate, Confirm, Execute — because that is how customers naturally make progress toward hiring a solution. The section order is not arbitrary; it mirrors the decision journey a hair stylist takes from recognizing a problem to committing to an education platform.

## Section-by-Section Decision Map

| Page Section | JTBD Stage | Framework Used | Why This Section Exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hero** (`id="hero"`) | Define | Outcome-Based Messaging Strategy (Marketing Frameworks #18) + Four-Dimensional Job Analysis — functional job dimension (Marketing Frameworks #17) | The headline "Build extension skills that make clients request you by name" addresses the functional job (career progress through technique mastery) rather than listing features. Outcome-Based Messaging says to lead with what the customer achieves, not what the product contains. The functional job dimension ensures the first thing a stylist reads is about their career outcome. |
| **Struggle Stack** (`id="struggle"`) | Define | Job Map Framework — Define stage (Foundational Frameworks) + JTBD Core Framework — Step 5: Identify struggles (Product Frameworks #3) | The Define stage says customers must name their goal and constraints before moving forward. Naming 5 specific stylist frustrations in second-person ("You learned one method from one educator...") validates the visitor's experience before any solution is offered. This is struggle identification — the visitor thinks "they understand my problem." |
| **Manifesto** (`id="manifesto"`) | Define | Four-Dimensional Job Analysis — emotional + social job dimensions (Marketing Frameworks #17) + JTBD Anti-Vanity Framework (Design Team #1) | The manifesto addresses the emotional job (feel confident learning without judgment) and the social/identity job (be part of a community that rejects gatekeeping). Anti-Vanity ensures this section does real work — it bridges identity frustration to MTAM's philosophy rather than being a decorative mission statement. |
| **Topics Grid** (`id="topics"`) | Locate | Outcome-Based Messaging Strategy (Marketing Frameworks #18) + JTBD for IA — Top Task Analysis (Design Team #3, #4) | Locate stage: the customer gathers resources and information. Each topic card uses outcome language ("Place beaded rows that stay secure and comfortable") not feature counts ("50+ video lessons"). IA Top Task Analysis determined these are the 6 topics stylists most need to locate when evaluating an education platform. |
| **Educator Grid** (`id="educators"`) | Locate | JTBD Core Framework — Step 6: Current solutions analysis (Product Frameworks #3) + Customer Success Language Model (Marketing Frameworks #16) | Stylists evaluate who teaches, not just what's taught. Showing real educators with specialization labels lets visitors locate the expert who matches their gap. The Customer Success Language Model converts credentials into emotionally resonant labels ("Texture Specialist" not "Certified Level 3"). |
| **Founders Section** (`id="founders"`) | Locate | Four-Dimensional Job Analysis — identity dimension (Marketing Frameworks #17) + Elevated Abstraction Strategy (Strategy Frameworks #2) | The founders section tells the origin story through struggle, not credentials. The identity dimension says customers want to feel aligned with who is behind the product. Elevated Abstraction pushes past "we have 20 years experience" to the deeper why: "we built this because the industry told us there was one right way — there isn't." |
| **Testimonials** (`id="testimonials"`) | Confirm | Customer Success Language Model (Marketing Frameworks #16) + Outcome-Based Messaging Strategy (Marketing Frameworks #18) | Confirm stage: the customer verifies everything is ready before committing. Testimonials describe specific practice changes ("I extended [appointment intervals] to ten weeks and my clients noticed the difference") not generic praise. Success Language rephrases outcomes into emotionally resonant proof. The visitor thinks "people like me got results." |
| **Pricing** (`id="pricing"`) | Confirm | Job Map Framework — Confirm stage (Foundational Frameworks) + Outcome-Driven Innovation — Opportunity Score logic (Strategy Frameworks #3) | Pricing is confirmation, not execution — the customer verifies the investment is justified. Value comparison anchoring ("$500-$2,500 per single certification" vs. "$105/month for everything") reframes cost as an opportunity score: high importance (learn techniques) + low current satisfaction (overpriced alternatives) = high opportunity. The "Most Popular" badge reduces decision friction. |
| **Final CTA** (`id="cta"`) | Execute | Customer Success Language Model (Marketing Frameworks #16) + Job Map Framework — Execute stage (Foundational Frameworks) | Execute stage: the customer performs the action that delivers core value. "Start Learning Today" uses job-completion language — it describes the action the customer takes, not the platform's action. The Customer Success Language Model converts "Subscribe Now" (platform verb) into "Start Learning" (customer verb). |
| **Footer** (`id="footer"`) | Execute | JTBD Anti-Vanity Framework (Design Team #1) + JTBD for IA (Design Team #3) | Anti-Vanity strips the footer to only what serves a job: copyright (legal requirement), Terms and Contact (trust signals). No social media links, no secondary navigation, no newsletter signup — each of those would be a vanity element that distracts from the completed job. The page ends clean. |

## Summary

Every section of the MTAM homepage demo traces back to a specific JTBD framework and principle. The page follows the Job Map's universal sequence — Define (hero, struggle, manifesto), Locate (topics, educators, founders), Confirm (testimonials, pricing), Execute (CTA, footer) — because this mirrors how customers naturally make progress. The section order is not arbitrary design preference; it is a deliberate application of how people move from recognizing a problem to committing to a solution.

A presenter can point to any section of the live demo and read the corresponding row in the table above to explain exactly which JTBD principle put that section there and why it exists in that position on the page.

---

## Frameworks Referenced

- **Job Map Framework (Universal Job Map)** — Foundational Frameworks Cheat Sheet. Provides the Define > Locate > Prepare > Confirm > Execute sequence that structures the entire page.
- **Four-Dimensional Job Analysis** — Marketing Frameworks #17. Analyzes the functional, emotional, social, and identity dimensions of the customer's job. Applied to hero (functional), manifesto (emotional + social), and founders (identity).
- **Outcome-Based Messaging Strategy** — Marketing Frameworks #18. Dictates that all copy leads with what the customer achieves, not what the product contains. Applied to hero, topics, and testimonials.
- **Customer Success Language Model** — Marketing Frameworks #16. Converts platform-centric language into customer-centric language. Applied to educators, testimonials, and the final CTA.
- **JTBD Anti-Vanity Framework** — Design Team Framework #1. Ensures every page element serves a customer job — no decorative sections, no vanity metrics. Applied to manifesto and footer.
- **JTBD for IA / Top Task Analysis** — Design Team Framework #3, #4. Organizes content by what customers most need to find, not by internal org structure. Applied to topics grid and footer.
- **JTBD Core Framework** — Product Framework #3. Provides the struggle identification methodology (Step 5) and current solutions analysis (Step 6). Applied to struggle stack and educator grid.
- **Elevated Abstraction Strategy** — Strategy Frameworks #2. Pushes messaging past surface-level claims to deeper emotional truths. Applied to founders section.
- **Outcome-Driven Innovation (ODI)** — Strategy Frameworks #3. Provides the opportunity score logic (importance vs. satisfaction) that frames pricing as high-opportunity. Applied to pricing section.

## Presentation Delivery Notes

### Venue Assumption
This demo assumes **WiFi is available** at the Chennai STYSTS presentation venue (per decision D-10). All fonts, icons, animation libraries, and the Tailwind CSS engine load from CDNs at runtime.

### Target Device
Chrome on a Windows laptop (per decision D-11). No cross-browser testing performed.

### CDN Dependencies
The HTML file requires internet access to load these external resources:

| Resource | CDN | Purpose |
|----------|-----|---------|
| Tailwind CSS v4 (Play CDN) | cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/@tailwindcss/browser@4 | Utility-first styling engine — compiles all class-based styles at runtime |
| GSAP 3.14.2 | cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/gsap@3.14.2 | Core animation library |
| GSAP ScrollTrigger 3.14.2 | cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/gsap@3.14.2 (ScrollTrigger plugin) | Scroll-driven entrance animations for each section |
| Google Fonts (DM Serif Display, Manrope) | fonts.googleapis.com / fonts.gstatic.com | Display serif + body sans-serif typography |
| Phosphor Icons v2.1.2 (regular + fill) | cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/@phosphor-icons/web@2.1.2 | UI icons (checkmarks, stars) in regular and fill weights |
| Educator/founder photos (9 images) | alpha.uscreencdn.com | Real MTAM educator and founder headshots from Uscreen CDN |
| Hero background image | alpha.uscreencdn.com | Full-bleed hero section background photo |

### Pre-Presentation Checklist
1. Connect laptop to venue WiFi
2. Open `slideshows/mtam-homepage-demo.html` in Chrome
3. Scroll through the full page to confirm all sections render (fonts loaded, icons visible, animations trigger on scroll)
4. Verify all educator photos appear (8 headshots in the educator grid + 1 large founder photo)
5. If any resource fails to load, check browser console (F12 > Console tab) for blocked URLs

### Fallback Plan (if WiFi unavailable)
- **Fonts:** Browser will fall back to system sans-serif and serif fonts. Layout will not break, but typography will look generic.
- **Icons:** Phosphor icons will not render. Icon placeholders will appear as empty space. Non-blocking — no layout shift.
- **Animations:** GSAP will not load. All sections will appear statically (visible immediately, no scroll animations). The page remains fully readable and scrollable.
- **Photos:** Educator and founder images will not load. Empty placeholder areas will appear. This is the most visually noticeable degradation.
- **Tailwind CSS:** This is the critical dependency. Without Tailwind's Play CDN, utility classes will not compile and the page will render as unstyled HTML with raw text. **Mitigation:** If WiFi is uncertain, run `npx @tailwindcss/cli -i input.css -o output.css` before the trip to pre-compile styles, then embed the output in a `<style>` tag. This step is NOT needed if WiFi is confirmed.

### Verdict
With WiFi confirmed at the venue, the demo is presentation-ready as-is. No offline compilation needed. The only risk is Uscreen CDN availability for photos — if MTAM's hosting has an outage, educator headshots will not appear, but all other page content (text, layout, icons, animations) will render normally.
